Paper Summary — Matteini et al. (2024)

  Title: "Alfvénic fluctuations in the expanding solar wind: Formation and radial evolution of spherical polarization"
  Journal: Physics of Plasmas 31, 032901 (2024)

  What the paper does

  Uses 2D hybrid (kinetic ions + fluid electrons) expanding-box simulations to investigate how spherical polarization — the observed state where $|B|$
  stays nearly constant despite large-amplitude fluctuations — forms and evolves during solar wind radial expansion.

  Key findings

  1. Spherical polarization emerges spontaneously from balanced Alfvénic turbulence ($\sigma_c = 0$) — the first kinetic demonstration of this. It works
  because large-scale structures self-organize into locally high-$|\sigma_c|$ patches where nonlinearities are weak.
  2. Switchbacks form naturally as the inevitable large-amplitude limit: when $\delta B / B_0 \sim 1$, the field-aligned component $\delta B_R$ must grow
  large enough to cause polarity reversals.
  3. A simple formula $\delta B_R \approx \delta B^2/(2B_m)$ (derived from the constant-$|B|$ constraint) quantitatively explains both simulations and
  PSP/Helios/Ulysses observations across 0.15–5 AU, with no free parameters.
  4. Saturation: when $\delta B \sim B$, the polarization-sphere aspect ratio $\delta B_R/\delta B_\perp$ freezes, consistent with Ulysses polar wind data.
