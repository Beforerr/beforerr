# Figure Guide — Matteini et al. (2024)

## Paper Figures (extracted from PDF)

| Image File | Figure | Caption |
|------------|--------|---------|
| `img-035.jpg` | **Fig. 1** | Cartoon showing evolution of transverse Alfvénic fluctuations into spherical polarization. Three panels: (left) small amplitude fluctuations with nearly constant B; (center) larger fluctuations where dB⊥ ≈ B₀ causing significant B variations; (right) spherical polarization where field-aligned dBk develops to keep B ≈ const. Switchbacks arise when dBk > B₀ causing polarity reversal. |
| `img-036.jpg` | **Fig. 2** | PSP first-perihelion data (0.15 AU) showing spherical polarization. Left: scatterplot of BR vs BN in RTN coordinates. Right: BR vs B⊥ showing circular shell of constant B ≈ 80 nT. Data from ≈6-hour interval with nearly constant magnetic field magnitude. |
| `img-037.jpg` | **Fig. 3** | Simulation overview at R = 5R₀. Left: map of in-plane fluctuation amplitude dB⊥ showing 2D turbulence vortical structures. Middle: power spectra of normalized fluctuations dB/B₀ (red), dv/VA (blue), |dB|/B₀ (orange). Bottom: magnetic spectrum compensated by k^(−5/3). Right: evolution of dB/B vs R (red); dashed line shows dB/B₀ ∝ R prediction; dash-dotted shows dB/B evolution. |
| `img-038.jpg` | **Fig. 4** | Radial profiles of magnetic field quantities. Top: total magnitude B (black), rms of total fluctuations dB (red), and radial dBR (blue). Bottom: dB/B (red), dB⊥/B (black), and dBR/B (blue) ratios vs R. Shows transition from R^(−2) scaling to saturation when dB ~ B. |
| `img-039.jpg` | **Fig. 5** | Evolution of magnetic field polarization in the (BT, BR) plane at different R (color-coded). Left: projections showing arc-like pattern evolving toward spherical shell projection. Right: same data normalized to local ⟨B⟩ = Bm, showing B_R vs dB⊥ with points lying on spherical shell of radius Bm. |
| `img-040.jpg` | **Fig. 6** | Cross-helicity analysis. Top: PDF of cos(θ) where θ is angle between db⊥ and dv⊥ at R = 5R₀. Middle: contour map of σc at R = 3R₀ showing patches of high \|σc\| (red/blue = ±1). Bottom: polarization Bx vs BR in subregion showing arc-shaped distribution for both σc > 0 (blue) and σc < 0 (red) within the same structure. |
| `img-041.jpg` | **Fig. 7** | dBR distribution evolution. Left: histogram of dBR normalized to local B₀ at different R (black→red). Initially narrow/symmetric, develops asymmetric tail toward negative dBR with expansion. Right: PSP observation — PDF of dBR (red) and dBN (black) at 0.15 AU, showing asymmetric tail and geometric cutoff at \|dB/Bm\| = 2. |
| `img-042.jpg` | **Fig. 8** | 1D cuts through simulation box. Left: BR/B₀ at different R — shows growing negative peaks (polarity reversals) as dB/B increases. Right: total B/⟨B⟩ at same R — much smaller variations. Largest B variations do NOT coincide with largest dBR reversals. |
| `img-043.jpg` | **Fig. 9** | Switchback examples at R = 6.4R₀. Two cases showing 1D cuts: BR (blue), B⊥ (red), and total B (black) normalized to local B₀. Small B variations throughout structure despite large BR reversals bounded by sharp rotational-discontinuity-like transitions. |
| `img-044.jpg` | **Fig. 10** | Verification of B_R² + B⊥² = Bm² constraint. Top: B⊥ intensity contour at t=700 (R = 2.4R₀). Bottom: cuts along x-axis showing anti-correlation between BR (blue) and B⊥ (red) such that total B (black) has much smaller modulation. |
| `img-045.jpg` | **Fig. 11** | Model verification. Comparison of predicted dBsR = ⟨dB²/2Bm⟩ (black) vs measured rms of dBR (cyan) vs R. Good agreement confirms the analytical prediction from constant-B constraint. |
