Below is a conceptual and technical comparison between BO-PBK and BO-Arbitrary.

0. One-sentence summary

BO-PBK is an analytic, distribution-aware eigen-solver optimized for kappa plasmas, whereas BO-Arbitrary is a universal but numerically heavier framework that approximates any distribution at the cost of efficiency and low-κ accuracy.

1. Core philosophical difference

BO-PBK and BO-Arbitrary solve the same kinetic dispersion relation, but they start from very different representations of the velocity distribution function (VDF).

Aspect BO-PBK BO-Arbitrary
Guiding idea Exploit analytic structure of PBK/KM/BM distributions Represent any gyrotropic distribution numerically
Distribution handling Closed-form PBK → rational susceptibility Arbitrary VDF → basis expansion + fitting
Target users Those who know the VDF is PBK-like Those with measured or irregular VDFs

In short: BO-PBK is a specialized, analytic solver; BO-Arbitrary is a universal numerical framework.

2. Distribution representation

BO-PBK

  • Assumes product-bi-kappa (PBK) structure from the start: [ f(v_, v_)=f_{()}(v),f_{()}(v)]

  • Uses exact modified plasma dispersion functions (Z_{}) and their closed-form rational expansions.

  • Loss-cone, drift, anisotropy are built in analytically.

  • Reduces exactly to:

    • KM when (_)
    • BM when (,)

Key consequence: No numerical fitting of the VDF is required; all coefficients are known analytically.

BO-Arbitrary

  • Starts from no assumed functional form.

  • Expands the distribution as [ f_s(v_,v_)={l,m} a{lm},l(v),u_m(v_)] using:

    • Hermite–Hermite (HH),
    • GPDF–Hermite (GH),
    • or GPDF–GPDF (GG) bases

  • Distribution quality depends on:

    • truncation order,
    • basis choice,
    • numerical conditioning.

Key consequence: It can represent anything, but never exactly—only to numerical accuracy.

3. Eigenvalue formulation and matrix size

BO-PBK

  • PBK susceptibility reduces to a compact rational form: [ {ij}={n,l}]

  • Results in a smaller eigenvalue matrix:

    • ~2–3× smaller than BO-KM,
    • much smaller than BO-Arbitrary for comparable accuracy

  • Especially efficient for large κ, where Hermite fitting becomes expensive.

BO-Arbitrary

  • Matrix size scales with: [ N (l_{}m_{}J)] where:

    • (l,m) are basis orders,
      1. is the number of J-poles in rational approximation.

  • High-κ or sharp features → large basis orders required.

Bottom line: BO-PBK is lean and analytic; BO-Arbitrary is flexible but heavy.

4. Accuracy for kappa-like distributions

This is one of the most important practical distinctions.

BO-PBK

  • Exact by construction for PBK/KM/BM.
  • No Gibbs oscillations.
  • No convergence ambiguity at low κ.
  • Retains correct power-law tails automatically.

BO-Arbitrary

  • Hermite bases are Maxwellian-centered.
  • Power-law tails require many modes.
  • Accuracy degrades notably for low κ (strong suprathermal tails) .

Implication: For κ ≲ 5, BO-PBK is significantly more reliable.

5. Physical interpretability

Aspect BO-PBK BO-Arbitrary
Parameters (T_,T_,,,,u) Expansion coefficients (a_{lm})
Direct physics meaning Yes Indirect
Sensitivity scans Straightforward Nontrivial

BO-PBK keeps the physics transparent; BO-Arbitrary prioritizes generality.

6. When should you use each?

Use BO-PBK if:

  • The plasma is reasonably described by PBK/KM/BM.

  • You care about:

    • κ-dependent instability thresholds,
    • anisotropy-driven modes,
    • loss-cone physics,
    • fast parameter scans.

  • You want speed + robustness.

Use BO-Arbitrary if:

  • The distribution:

    • comes from spacecraft data,
    • has plateaus, shoulders, or holes,
    • cannot be parameterized analytically.

  • You are willing to trade efficiency for universality.
